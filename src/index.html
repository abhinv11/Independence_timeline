1613

East India Company Arrives

The British East India Company established its first trading post in India, marking the beginning of British commercial presence. This seemingly innocent trade relationship would eventually evolve into centuries of colonial rule, fundamentally changing the course of Indian history.

Colonial Era Trade
1757

Battle of Plassey

The decisive Battle of Plassey on June 23, 1757, marked the beginning of British political dominance in India. Robert Clive's victory over Siraj-ud-Daulah established British control over Bengal and paved the way for colonial expansion across the subcontinent.

Military Bengal
1857

The Great Revolt

The Indian Rebellion of 1857, spanning from May 10, 1857 to July 8, 1859, was the first major uprising against British rule. Led by brave heroes like Rani Lakshmibai, it marked the beginning of organized resistance to colonial oppression.

Rebellion Rani Lakshmibai
1885

Indian National Congress Founded

On December 28, 1885, the Indian National Congress was established, marking the birth of organized political movement for independence. This historic moment laid the foundation for India's struggle for self-governance and democratic representation.

Politics Congress
1906

All India Muslim League

The All India Muslim League was established on December 30, 1906, to represent the political interests of Muslims in British India. This organization would play a crucial role in the independence movement and the eventual partition of India.

1915

Gandhi Returns to India

On January 9, 1915, Mahatma Gandhi returned to India from South Africa, bringing with him the philosophy of Satyagraha (non-violent resistance). His arrival marked the beginning of a new era in India's freedom struggle, emphasizing peaceful protest and civil disobedience.

Gandhi Satyagraha
1919

Rowlatt Act Passed

In February 1919, the British government passed the repressive Rowlatt Act, allowing detention without trial. This draconian law sparked widespread protests across India and intensified the demand for complete independence from British rule.

1919

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

The horrific Jallianwala Bagh massacre on April 13, 1919, where General Dyer ordered troops to fire on unarmed civilians, became a turning point in India's freedom struggle. This brutal act galvanized the nation against British rule and united Indians in their quest for independence.

1920

Non-Cooperation Movement

From December 1920 to August 1922, Gandhi led the Non-Cooperation Movement, calling for peaceful resistance against British rule. This mass movement saw millions of Indians boycotting British goods, institutions, and services, demonstrating the power of unified non-violent protest.

1930

Independence Day Declaration

On January 26, 1930, the Indian National Congress declared Purna Swaraj (complete independence) as India's goal. This historic declaration marked the formal demand for complete freedom from British rule, setting the stage for intensified freedom struggle.

1930

Dandi Salt March

From March 12 to April 5, 1930, Gandhi led the historic Salt March to Dandi, walking 240 miles to protest the British salt monopoly. This symbolic act of defiance inspired millions and became one of the most iconic moments of India's freedom struggle.

Salt March Civil Disobedience
1932

Poona Pact Signed

On September 24, 1932, the Poona Pact was signed between Gandhi and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, ensuring reserved seats for depressed classes within the general electorate. This agreement addressed the issue of separate electorates and maintained Hindu unity in the freedom struggle.

1937

Provincial Elections

In February 1937, provincial elections were held under the Government of India Act 1935. The Indian National Congress achieved significant victories, forming governments in several provinces and demonstrating the people's faith in democratic self-governance.

1939

World War II Declaration

On September 3, 1939, Britain declared war on Germany and automatically included India without consulting Indian leaders. This unilateral decision sparked outrage and led to the resignation of Congress ministries, further straining British-Indian relations.

1940

Pakistan Resolution

On March 23, 1940, the All India Muslim League passed the Pakistan Resolution in Lahore, formally demanding separate nations for Muslims in the Indian subcontinent. This resolution laid the foundation for the eventual partition of India.

1942

Quit India Movement

On August 8, 1942, Gandhi launched the Quit India Movement with the powerful slogan "Do or Die." This final major movement of the freedom struggle demanded immediate British withdrawal from India and marked the beginning of the end of colonial rule.

Quit India Do or Die
1947

Indian Independence Act

On July 18, 1947, the British Parliament passed the Indian Independence Act, formally ending British rule in India. This historic legislation provided for the partition of British India into two independent dominions: India and Pakistan.

1947

Independence Achieved

On the night of August 14-15, 1947, India finally achieved independence from British rule. As the clock struck midnight, Jawaharlal Nehru delivered his famous "Tryst with Destiny" speech, marking the birth of free India after centuries of colonial rule.

Freedom Tryst with Destiny
1947

Partition and Migration

The partition of India in 1947-1948 led to one of the largest mass migrations in human history. Millions of people crossed borders between the newly formed nations of India and Pakistan, marking both the joy of independence and the tragedy of division.

1948

Gandhi's Martyrdom

On January 30, 1948, Mahatma Gandhi was assassinated by Nathuram Godse in New Delhi. The Father of the Nation's tragic death shocked the world and marked the end of an era, but his principles of non-violence and truth continue to inspire generations.

1950

Republic Day

On January 26, 1950, India became a republic with the adoption of its Constitution. This date was chosen to honor the 1930 Declaration of Independence, completing India's journey from a British colony to a sovereign, democratic republic.

Republic Constitution
